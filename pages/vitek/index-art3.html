YouTube, (still) the ultimate music platform

In today's merciless, constatly evolving world of technologies and platforms, the saying "old but gold" does not apply anymore. Just look at social media like ICQ or MySpace, or search engines like Yahoo or Ask.

One of the biggest exceptions is the now 12-year-old video/audio sharing platform YouTube. When it comes to music listening, there are several platforms offering purely music-listening-taylored user experience, yet still, based on my super accurate research, 86.7% of people use YouTube to listen to their jams.

YouTube's probably biggest and a well-known asset is that you can find literally everything there. Indie remixes, covers, mashups or mixes, YouTube's got it all! Another thing, which you might not be familiar with (unless you are, or have read my article!), the official or the community-well-done uploads on YouTube totally smash for example SoundCloud's 128kbps Mp3s in quality by implementing the so much better Opus audio format.

Now we are getting to the main point of this article. A reeally both-sided part of YouTube is the huge community power it wields. The way some people think doesn't cease to amaze me, both in the good AND the bad way. Sometimes it's words of praise, sometimes it's just salt and roast and sometimes it's pure randomness.
