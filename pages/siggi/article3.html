Budget Home Bar

Going to the bar to meet up with friends over some drinks is essential to keep from going crazy as a student. But it can be very costly and most students are living on a tight budget, sometimes we will rather stay at home and drink cheap beer from the can to save money. There is a certain appeal to sit in your living room with your friends and get wasted on cheap pilsners, no doubt about that. But what if I told you, you could have the bar experience in your living room without having to shell out a fortune? Let me introduce you to the Budget Home Bar. You just need a couple of essentials, a little bit of practice and you can impress your friends with some tasty cocktails and upgrade your drinking game.

First of all you need to get some tools, there is no reason to buy some top of the line equipment, you only need the basics. A shaker,strainer, stirring spoon and a jigger (I recommend getting one that measures in ounces since most of the recipes you find online are in ounces). You can buy a bartending set in most household stores for under 200 kr. If you have some extra money laying around you could also invest in different sized glassware but usually what you have on hand will do fine.

Let‘s get to the exciting part, what types of liqour and what brands you should get. To make the most popular and simple drinks you are going to need Vodka, Bourbon, Gin, Aged Rum, Tequila, Dry Vermouth, Martini Rosso, bitters and maybe Triple Sec. Couple those basics with some mixers (club soda, tonic water, ginger ale), fresh citrus and a couple juices, some clean-tasting ice, and some 1:1 simple syrup, you can make hundreds of cocktails.

What should you get?

Vodka

  • Smirnoff (125kr)
  • Absolut (150kr)
  • Finlandia (135kr)

Gin

Tequila

Conclusion

Your budget home bar start-up is gonna cost you between 700 and 1600 kr. depending on what you buy. Of course you don‘t have to buy everything at once but as soon as you get started your gonna save money from not having to buy drinks at the bar and get a thousand cool points from your friends. If you just want to be able to make the most basic cocktails you should buy a bottle of vodka, gin, rum and bourbon. Then you can make loads of simple syrup (equal parts sugar and water) to always have on hand and stock up on club soda, tonic, lime and lemons. Now you should be good to go!
