5 Tips To Prevent Neck Pain During Computer Work

As a designer, you can spend hours a day in front of computers without thinking of its impact on your body. Sometimes, You might notice that your neck is getting stiff and sore, and you might even have a slight headache. That can be explained by the fact that you might be physically stressing your body daily without realizing it. That can be caused by extending your wrists, slouching, sitting without foot support; to look at poorly placed monitors. Here are few tips for you that can easily help you avoid neck pain while using your computer.

1) Getting Your Chair Right.

Every time you work, take time to adjust workstations that aren't feeling quite right while using them. This will help you minimize awkward and frequently performed movements.

2) Reduce prolonged computer usage time whenever possible.

3) Make sure your computer monitor is at eye level.

Ensure the viewing screen is directly in front, approximately arms-length away, with the top of the screen positioned slightly below eyelevel.

4) "Change your position every 15 minutes"

says Dr. Dennerlein, an adjunct professor of ergonomics and safety at the Harvard School of Public Health

5) Move!

Take a break every 15 minutes to stretch and/or walk around
