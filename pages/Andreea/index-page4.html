What do teachers search in future students?

-At Business Academy Aarhus-

If you are in search for an undergraduate program, or just thinking about the criteria for getting the teachers to like you, then this article is for you!


I’ve just interviewed Maria Louise Bendixen, the class team leader and the teacher for business and communication to find out more details about this subject.

If you are already a student then you might be familiar with the application process, the requirements from universities and the interview. These are the main aspects of applying for studying abroad.


Grades and subjects list.

At Business Academy Aarhus, they first ask you to send a list with your grades and subjects during high school. This thing is mainly used to understand if you’re familiar with the chosen filled or to see if you have done this before. The grade list is an overview of your involvement in projects during high school.


The interview.

After this step, you’ll need to schedule an interview. During the interview they test your English level, your way of thinking and discuss some aspects of your personal statement that needed clarification. Furthermore, teachers usually search in you the main aspects of the program (ex. team working, ambition, communication skills etc.). Basically, after the interview, they need to have an overall image of you fitting or not into the chosen program.


Applying as a Dane.

The process for the Danish people is different. For an AP degree here at the academy they only need their grades from high school. An interesting fact about the academy’s program is that after finishing an AP degree either in English or Danish, the Top-up degree at the Academy will be taught only in English.
