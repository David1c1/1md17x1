UNIVERSITY VS ACADEMY

-In Denmark-

Definition.

In the definition of the “university” we can find out this word is related to “theoretical learning”, “concept development”, “written exams”. On the other hand, thinking about the definition of the “academy” we’ll soon find out the main difference between these two. An academy brings out the practice, projects, team work and applying the knowledge.


The university is “think”, while the academy is “do”.

Many people tend to confuse these two different terms, or they don’t fully understand them. One of the most asked question might be “What’s the difference between the bachelor degree you get after finishing any of them?”
Well, the difference doesn’t stay in the number of ECTS points, but in the trade you get after graduation.



Example.

Let’s take the example of architecture. After the graduation the architect (the one from university) will develop the concept and plan the details while the engineer (the one from the academy) will do the actual work.

Master degrees.

Usually, people misunderstand this kind of education. Of course, you won’t get a master degree in mathematics if you just got the bachelor degree in web design.



It’s all based on the skills you have and the subjects you’ve already learnt about. After an academic bachelor program you might need to do some other courses in order to increase your skills before getting into a master degree program.



Time.

Another difference between university and academy is the amount of time needed to get your bachelor degree.
During the academy you get the chance to do an internship in order to work with actual companies, and test the knowledge you have in real life situations. Therefore, completing your education at an academy will take 3 and a half a year, while getting your degree at a university will take 3 years.
