Price Guide:
Spoon logic

Most commen item in stores

The wooden spoon or as they call it in danish “Træske”, is probably the most commen item you’ll find in any second-hand shop here in Denmark.
It’s been used since the dark ages (if not earlier) and it still serves it’s purpose here 1000 years later.

Pricing in second-hand stores

Most people that go to second-hand stores would probably argue that they have a sense of pricing in the store if not just for special parts of it.
But what of those whom find it hard to decide rather or not the pricing is high or low in a special shop.

Mass production

Træsken (wooden spoon) was mass produced during the late 1800 and early 1900 and have ever since been produced heavily.
Due to it’s practical look, useful function and cheap martial. It’s held in high regard to most danes.

Deciding pricing based on a spoon

For us who find it somewhat difficult there’s a simple solution. Find a wooden spooon (the size you’d use for steering in a soup)
Once you’ve found it, act as if you want to buy it - then based on that price you’ll find out how badly priced things are in the shop

Types of spoons

With the amount of spoons produced there’s also bound to be many types of them as well, in fact some are rare and vaulable.
Cooking and steering spoons are most commen, but eating spoons are rare and considered neat, they might fetch a good hagling price.

Examples of spoon pricing:
Free = Low pricing
1 krone = medium pricing
2 - 3 krones = high pricing
3 > krones = Unfair pricing
