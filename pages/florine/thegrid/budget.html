Budgeting Checklist

With uni books and stationary, housing, food and the occasional night out, it can get difficult to find your way thought the jungle of finance and budgeting.  

We've created a budgeting checklist with 10 tips of what you could do to save money, so that at the end of the day, you end up with surplus that could go into your savings.

 
  • Download a budgeting app

We really like the app Wally for that, as it keeps a fantastic oversight of all our current spending and even has a function to put you on a budget an calculate your daily spendings to achieve that goal.

 
  • Automate your savings

We think that every budget should include putting something in your savings account. Depending on your monthly income, you can automate the amount you would like to save every month and have it taken straight from your regular spending account, so you won't ever see and, therefore, won't ever miss the money.

 
  • Pay off the balance of your credit card each month

Leaving your credit card bill unpaid for a longer period of time can not only ruin your mood, it is also really bad for your credit, which is an important factor if you want get a bank loan in the future.

  • Spend your money on quality items

This might seem counter intuitive, as quality things might, in the beginning, cost more. Let me explain it with a personal story. When I started my higher educational program, Granpa Hofmann would always say: "You are too poor for cheap things now", meaning that if I wanted to save money, I needed to invest in things that would last me a long time and that I would get great value of.

 
  • Speak your mind

Another great budgeting tip inspired by Grandpa Hofmann's advice is to say no to activities when you know you can't afford them. There is no shame in wanting to save and as Grandpa Hofmann would say: "It's either open your mouth or open your wallet".

The Ultimate Grocery Shopping Guide for Students

As students we have usually don't want to spend much time on the thought of what to buy when, where and how.

Eating healthy and nutricious meals while being cost efficient requires some preparation in order for it to become a convenient routine.

We have created list of essential shopping tools and a list of things to buy in the beginning of the month in order to prevent unhealthy eating habits at the end of the month. We present to you:

 

The Ultimate Shopping Guide for Students

 

What to take when going on a food shopping spree:

 
  • Washable, reusable bags to put your groceries in (colourful pillowcases work just as well).
  • A few mason jars or something similar to put flour, grain, spices, liquid like soap or oil in. Try to find a reusable wine carrier to carry them.
  • A few small cloth bags for nuts and herbs.
What to buy in bulk at the beginning of the month:

"Accessorize" your main ingredients every week with different fresh vegetables and fruit in order to keep your diet balanced and your cooking experience fun.
